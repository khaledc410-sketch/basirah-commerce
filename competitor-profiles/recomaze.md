# Recomaze — competitor profile

**URL:** https://recomaze.ai/product  
**Shopify:** https://apps.shopify.com/recomaze-ai  
**Updated:** 2026-07-16  
**Role in this benchmark:** closest direct product analogue to Basirah's “visibility engine + sales agent” vision

## At a glance

| Dimension | Observation |
|---|---|
| Positioning | AI commerce stack combining answer-engine visibility, catalog remediation, and an on-site sales agent |
| Shopify proof | 5.0/5 from 6 reviews in the reviewed listing |
| Launch | 5 August 2024 |
| Entry | Free credit tier; $39, $109, and $399 paid tiers; seven-day trial |
| Best capability | The clearest one-install, two-engine story with diagnostics adjacent to actions |
| Main weakness | Very small proof base, opaque credit economics, and claims that are more aggressive than the public evidence |

## Positioning and journey

Recomaze addresses almost the same strategic loop as Basirah: make the catalog understandable to AI systems, measure where the brand loses, improve the content, and deploy a catalog-aware storefront sales agent.

Its marketing compresses onboarding into a memorable time sequence: install immediately, fix the catalog in the first hour, launch the sales agent on day one, appear in indexes during the first week, and improve against competitors over the first month. Those are positioning claims rather than verified universal outcomes, but the structure is excellent.

## Report and product flow

The product gallery showed:

1. Share of voice by model and product.
2. Query-level win/loss against competitors.
3. A recommended fix next to each lost query.
4. Bulk product rewrites with before/after views and a publish-all action.
5. Eighty-four weekly diagnostic checks and a prioritized readiness list.
6. A product-page sales agent that can recommend an item, remember a size preference, and present add-to-cart.

The free audit is a strong acquisition mechanism: URL and email unlock ten queries, competitor comparison, citations, share of voice, and recommendations. The email requirement creates friction, but it also turns a high-intent diagnosis into a lead.

## Landing page, marketing, and design

- Black background, magenta/purple gradients, dense UI panels, and urgent category-creation copy.
- The fear/opportunity frame—AI is already shopping, so can it find the merchant?—creates immediate relevance.
- Product screenshots communicate action, not just measurement: apply, rewrite, publish, and add to cart.
- The design feels technically ambitious and fast, but less calm and trustworthy than Basirah's evidence-led Arabic report.

## What Recomaze does especially well

- Expresses the visibility-to-sales flywheel as one product.
- Makes lost queries and competitor evidence actionable.
- Shows before/after changes and bulk workflows.
- Connects the diagnostic engine to the storefront agent in one story.
- Uses a free audit as both education and acquisition.

## What Basirah should learn—and avoid

Learn:

- Present the full loop in five verbs: diagnose, prove, fix, sell, measure.
- Place a proposed action beside every finding.
- Build a public audit that reveals useful value before asking for installation.
- Show the sales agent in realistic product-page context.

Avoid:

- Credit pricing that forces merchants to translate credits into value.
- One-click bulk publishing without checksums, review, versioning, and rollback.
- Strong claims about indexing, competitor decline, or model recommendations without reproducible evidence.
