# Chatty — competitor profile

**URL:** https://chatty.net/  
**Shopify:** https://apps.shopify.com/chatty  
**Updated:** 2026-07-16  
**Role in this benchmark:** scaled Shopify distribution, low-friction entry, and demo-led AI sales positioning

## At a glance

| Dimension | Observation |

## Positioning and customer journey

Chatty did not begin with the whole “AI salesperson” story. Its entry wedge was a job Shopify merchants already understood: FAQs, order questions, WhatsApp contact, and live support. It then expanded into catalog-aware answers, product recommendations, proactive engagement, cart recovery, analytics, and omnichannel operations.

The current journey is simple:

1. Discover the product in the Shopify App Store.
2. Install on a free plan without engineering work.
3. Synchronize products and configure the widget, FAQs, and channels.
4. Use support automation first and receive hands-on setup help if needed.
5. Expand to product recommendations, proactive selling, additional channels, and attribution.
6. Upgrade when conversation volume, catalog size, team needs, or analytics justify it.

This progression is strategically important: the initial support job creates daily habit and data before the merchant is asked to believe a larger AI-sales promise.

## Report, analytics, and product flow

Chatty's product is organized around conversations more than formal AI-feasibility reporting. Its commercial value is made legible through AI conversation volume, support resolution, product interactions, and attributed orders. It also covers email, Instagram, WhatsApp, Messenger, and mobile operations, which makes the agent part of an operating inbox instead of a single website widget.

Its best conversion device is the public interactive demo:

- merchants choose fashion, furniture, electronics, beauty, or sports;
- they enter a realistic storefront;
- the widget opens with category-specific buying questions;
- they experience the interaction before installing.

That is more persuasive than a gallery of screenshots because it lets the merchant project the agent into their own category.

## Landing page, marketing, and design

- Bright yellow and indigo gradient, friendly rounded type, and playful social-proof badges.
- A compact centered hero with a free CTA and a demo CTA.
- Marketplace proof and adoption are close to the primary promise.
- The product feels accessible to a small merchant, not like enterprise software requiring a sales process.
- Self-reported revenue figures differ between the homepage and demo. The company may be measuring different concepts, but the labels are not clear enough for a trust-sensitive product.

## What Chatty does especially well

- Uses a free tier and Shopify distribution to remove the first adoption barrier.
- Makes customer success and fast support part of the product experience.
- Gives merchants a self-guided, category-specific demo.
- Expands from a narrow, frequent support use case into sales and analytics.
- Offers channel breadth, including WhatsApp, which is highly relevant to GCC commerce.

## What Basirah should learn—and avoid

Learn:

- Let a merchant feel the agent in a familiar vertical before asking for installation.
- Use support quality and merchant onboarding as launch features, not back-office operations.
- Add WhatsApp handoff early for the GCC market.
- Earn the sales-agent expansion through a dependable first job.

Avoid:

- Mixing differently defined revenue numbers without methodology.
- Letting a broad inbox roadmap dilute Basirah's stronger Arabic commerce and evidence proposition.
- Claiming sales impact until Salla events, orders, and attribution are reconciled.
