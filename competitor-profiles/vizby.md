# Vizby — competitor profile

**URL:** https://www.vizby.ai/  
**Shopify:** https://apps.shopify.com/ai-blog-post-generator-1  
**Updated:** 2026-07-16  
**Role in this benchmark:** the clearest operational AI-visibility flow from diagnosis to approved store change

## At a glance

| Dimension | Observation |
|---|---|
| Positioning | AI visibility platform that diagnoses, prioritizes, implements, and measures |
| Shopify proof | Built for Shopify; 5.0/5 from 30 reviews in the reviewed listing |
| Launch | 23 October 2025 |
| Entry | $29/month; four paid tiers up to $499; 14-day trial |
| Best capability | Turns visibility findings into an approval queue and an activity log |
| Main weakness | New evidence base and ambitious claims across off-site channels, citations, and automated publishing |

## Positioning and journey

Vizby's most important line is not its score; it is the claim that most tools stop at a score and Vizby starts there. The product narrative consistently follows four stages:

1. Understand visibility across answer engines, prompts, citations, and competitors.
2. Receive a prioritized action plan.
3. Implement changes inside Shopify.
4. Measure sessions, conversions, and revenue.

This makes it a strong benchmark for Basirah's report-to-action transition.

## Report and action flow

The reviewed dashboard showed platform tabs, topic coverage, citations, a score, and a prompt-level table. More importantly, each finding could lead to a concrete task. The merchant can inspect proposed content or structured-data changes and approve, revise, reject, or save them to Shopify.

The activity log records past and queued actions, whether they were approved or auto-published, and what remains pending. The catalog view presents per-product scores and actions such as fixing JSON-LD or enhancing content.

The key pattern is:

```text
evidence → prioritized finding → proposed diff → merchant approval → publish → activity log → re-test
```

That is the product behavior Basirah currently describes in reports but has not yet completed as a verified production workflow.

## Landing page, marketing, and design

- Deep forest green, understated premium typography, large negative space, and editorial polish.
- Direct, memorable promise: become the brand AI recommends.
- Shopify installation and demo paths are both visible.
- The four-stage product flow is easier to understand than a long feature inventory.
- Social proof and a vendor case study make the new category feel more concrete, although both need independent scrutiny.

## What Vizby does especially well

- Converts a diagnostic product into an operating system for changes.
- Makes approval, revision, queued work, and activity history visible.
- Links query evidence to catalog/content remediation.
- Packages complex AI-visibility work as four merchant decisions rather than a technical audit.
- Uses tier limits to translate model coverage, prompt volume, audits, and content output into plans.

## What Basirah should learn—and avoid

Learn:

- Put the work queue next to the report, with owner, impact, status, evidence, and a proposed change.
- Add diff review, approve/reject, publish state, version history, and rollback before enabling automation.
- Use an activity log so the merchant can see what changed and why.
- Compress visibility into score/coverage, losing questions, evidence/competitors, and next actions.

Avoid:

- Blending readiness with observed visibility or implying causal control over model recommendations.
- Automating off-site or store changes before stable permissions, contracts, and rollback exist.
- Treating provider unavailability as a negative score.
