# Basirah competitive benchmark and action plan

**Prepared:** 2026-07-16  
**Scope:** Shopify AI sales agents and AI-visibility/readiness apps, benchmarked against the current Basirah product, Arabic landing page, report, visibility dashboard, widget studio, MVP backlog, and Salla listing draft.

## Executive conclusion

Basirah should not try to beat Shopify apps on generic chatbot breadth. Its defensible position is:

> **The Arabic evidence-to-revenue system for Salla merchants: diagnose what AI systems and shoppers cannot understand, show the evidence, approve the fix, sell with grounded answers, and measure the commercial result.**

The current product already has the hardest trust foundations:

- Arabic-first, RTL product and GCC/Salla context ignored by the reviewed competitors;
- a clear distinction between readiness and observed AI visibility;
- evidence, confidence, coverage, and methodology in the report;
- deterministic product filtering before generated explanation;
- price and stock provenance plus safety behavior for sensitive categories;
- a coherent loop from scan to findings, plan, content, rescan, conversations, and opportunities;
- a polished landing page, deep report, and strong widget-brand studio.

The gap is not another dashboard redesign. The gap is a production-proven loop:

```text
free scan → evidence → approved fix → Salla publish/install → grounded conversation
          → verified cart/order → revenue attribution → case study → marketplace review
```

Until that loop works with real stores, competitors will look more complete because they can show installation, actions, assisted sales, app reviews, and customer outcomes—even when Basirah's methodology is more trustworthy.

## What each competitor is good at

### Chatty: distribution, habit, and product experience

Chatty's advantage is not a more sophisticated report. It has a low-friction free entry, a familiar support wedge, a wide communication workflow, strong public reviews, and responsive support. Its category-specific public demo is particularly effective: a merchant can choose fashion, beauty, or another vertical and experience relevant prompts in a realistic store.

The lesson for Basirah is to make the product feel real before requiring installation. The warning is that breadth can become a generic helpdesk, and commercial metrics lose trust when differently defined numbers appear without clear methodology.

### Rep AI: premium sales execution and commercial legibility

Rep does the best job of connecting proactive shopper behavior, conversation, discovery, cart actions, sales, support outcomes, and structured customer intelligence. Its editorial landing page, high-visibility proof, dual trial/demo CTA, risk reversal, integrations, and white-glove onboarding make it feel like a revenue platform rather than a widget.

The lesson is to show commercial outcomes and make onboarding part of the offer. Basirah should not copy unverified uplift claims or complicated visitor-based pricing.

### Vizby: diagnosis becomes an operating workflow

Vizby's differentiator is the transition from score to action. It shows query evidence, proposed remediation, approval/revision/rejection, publish state, queued tasks, an activity log, and re-testing. Its landing page reduces a complex category to four stages: understand, prioritize, implement, measure.

The lesson is that Basirah's excellent report must become a merchant work queue. The warning is to retain Basirah's stricter distinction between readiness, observation, and causal commercial effect.

### Recomaze: the closest two-engine story

Recomaze shows AI visibility and an on-site sales agent as one installation. Its query win/loss, competitor evidence, adjacent fixes, bulk before/after rewrites, weekly checks, and product-page sales agent make the loop easy to see. The free audit is also a strong lead magnet.

The lesson is packaging: diagnose, fix, publish, sell, measure. The warnings are limited marketplace proof, credit-cost opacity, aggressive time-to-impact claims, and risky bulk publishing.

## Where Basirah is already stronger

### 1. A credible regional wedge

All four benchmarks are English-first and Shopify-centered. Basirah is built around Arabic buying language, RTL operation, Saudi/GCC merchant expectations, and Salla. This is not a localization detail; Arabic product naming, dialect, attribute ambiguity, shipping questions, COD, and WhatsApp behavior change both visibility and sales-agent quality.

### 2. The most defensible measurement model

The current Basirah visibility screen separates readiness from observed mentions and labels the observation method. It does not convert unavailable provider data into failure. The report shows confidence, coverage, evidence, owner, and recommended fix. That is materially more trustworthy than collapsing a store audit and answer-engine appearance into one mysterious score.

### 3. Grounded sales behavior

Basirah's product filtering happens before natural-language generation. Price and stock have provenance, and sensitive product questions have safety behavior. This is the correct architecture for a sales agent that must not invent products, availability, price, or health claims.

### 4. Product and visual quality

The Arabic landing page is clean and focused. The report has excellent depth. The widget studio combines identity, welcome copy, appearance, safety, live preview, and launch tests. The product already looks more credible than many early-stage AI tools.

### 5. A coherent strategic loop

The demo connects readiness findings, a 30/60/90 plan, content opportunities, rescanning, customer conversations, and missing-information opportunities. The pieces are not random features; they can become a compounding data loop.

## What is missing

### Product and proof gaps

1. **Verified Salla production loop.** Marketplace approval, live OAuth, real catalog sync, signed widget installation, origin controls, and operational monitoring remain launch gates.
2. **Real event and order reconciliation.** Demo metrics are honestly labeled, but there is no defensible direct/influenced order attribution yet.
3. **Observed visibility evidence at production quality.** Current views demonstrate the method but do not yet provide a repeatable, compliant, timestamped provider cohort with failure coverage.
4. **Report-to-publish workflow.** Findings and drafts exist; merchants still need a diff, approve/reject, publish/export, version, checksum, rollback, activity log, and re-test workflow.
5. **Proactive selling behavior.** The agent needs contextual PDP questions and carefully scoped triggers for hesitation, exit intent, comparison, and cart state.
6. **Human and WhatsApp handoff.** Chatty demonstrates why channel continuity matters. In the GCC, WhatsApp should precede a broad omnichannel roadmap.
7. **Production integrations.** Catalog and orders come first; helpdesk, WhatsApp, CRM/marketing, and analytics follow based on design-partner demand.
8. **Public customer evidence.** There are no verified live-store case studies, marketplace reviews, outcome benchmarks, or merchant quotes yet.

### Landing-page and marketing gaps

1. **The page asks the visitor to understand two products at once.** The current copy is strong, but visibility and sales-agent capabilities create cognitive load. The free checker should be the entry story; the sales agent is the expansion after the first finding.
2. **No public real-store report.** A sanitized sample with evidence, findings, accepted fixes, and a rescan would make the category concrete.
3. **No self-guided vertical agent demo.** Beauty, perfume, and gifts would show Arabic buying questions better than a generic widget preview.
4. **Limited public proof near the hero.** Competitors place ratings, logos, results, customer quotes, trials, and setup promises at the decision point.
5. **No validated risk reversal for Commerce.** The free scan helps, but the agent plan needs a pilot or assisted onboarding proposition until time-to-value is proven.
6. **Multiple stories across `/ar`, `/demo`, and the Salla listing draft.** The public funnel should use one sequence and reserve `/demo` as a clearly labeled sandbox.
7. **No repeatable content and proof engine.** Basirah needs merchant case studies, an Arabic AI-commerce benchmark, comparison pages, and a library of real Arabic buying questions.

### Pricing gap

The SAR 1,499 Commerce price is approximately a premium Shopify-agent price. It can be justified if it includes Arabic category expertise, white-glove setup, evidence reporting, grounded selling, and verified revenue measurement. It will feel expensive if presented as only a widget next to Chatty's $19.99–$199 tiers or Rep's roughly $99 entry.

Do not respond by automatically lowering the price. First clarify the package:

- what is installed and configured;
- which channels and catalog size are included;
- how many conversations or active shoppers are covered;
- which monthly reports and implementation actions are included;
- what evidence the merchant receives;
- what assisted onboarding or success support is guaranteed.

## Recommended landing-page story

Use one primary journey:

1. **Problem:** customers ask AI and storefront agents before buying; incomplete Arabic product facts make the merchant invisible or hard to recommend.
2. **Free action:** enter the Salla store URL and receive the first evidence-backed finding.
3. **Proof:** show the exact missing fact, affected buying question, confidence, and why it matters.
4. **Action:** preview the proposed fix and the merchant approval workflow.
5. **Selling:** demonstrate the same improved facts powering a grounded Arabic product recommendation.
6. **Measurement:** show direct versus influenced cart/order evidence and a before/after rescan.
7. **Trust:** explain source provenance, unsupported-claim blocking, availability labeling, human handoff, and merchant approval.

Recommended hero CTA structure:

- Primary: **افحص متجرك مجانًا**
- Secondary: **شاهد تقرير متجر حقيقي**
- Tertiary text link: **جرّب وكيلًا لمتجر عطور**

Only add marketplace badges, setup-time promises, ROI numbers, and customer logos after they are real and permissioned.

## 16-week execution plan

### Phase 0 — sharpen the funnel (week 1–2)

**Goal:** make the existing product understandable and testable before large engineering work.

- Use one promise everywhere: free diagnosis → first approved fix → grounded sales agent → measured result.
- Keep the current checker-led hero; add a public sample report and a vertical agent demo.
- Build three seeded self-guided demos from existing product data: perfume, beauty, and gifts.
- Turn the Salla listing gallery from a feature inventory into a five-scene story: diagnose, evidence, fix, sell, measure.
- Keep `/demo` explicitly labeled as sandbox/demo and do not let it compete with the primary `/ar` funnel.
- Instrument the acquisition funnel: landing, scan started, preview seen, lead/order, report opened, Salla connected, catalog ready, widget tested, widget live.

**Exit criteria:** a new visitor can explain the product in one sentence, inspect a real-style report, and experience one vertical agent without a sales call.

### Phase 1 — prove the Salla loop (week 2–6)

**Goal:** produce verified merchant value, not more demo completeness.

- Complete Salla development-store OAuth, catalog synchronization, signed storefront widget, origin allowlisting, and marketplace requirements.
- Begin with a safe “view product” flow; add cart actions only after the installed storefront contract is verified.
- Implement real event/order reconciliation with separate direct and influenced definitions.
- Recruit three to five design partners in perfume, beauty, and gifting.
- Provide white-glove setup and keep a correction log for agent answers and catalog gaps.
- Collect time-to-catalog-ready, five-test pass rate, time-to-first-correct-recommendation, recommendation click-through, cart events, reconciled orders, support handoffs, and merchant feedback.

**Exit criteria:** at least three live stores, reproducible installation, verified events/orders, and the first permissioned case study. Do not claim revenue lift before this gate.

### Phase 2 — turn the report into work (week 4–8)

**Goal:** make Basirah operational like Vizby while preserving better evidence.

- Reduce the main visibility view to four merchant decisions: score/coverage, top losing questions, observed evidence/competitors, and actions.
- Add a remediation queue with impact, evidence, confidence, owner, status, and source component.
- Add proposed diffs with approve, revise, reject, export/publish, version history, checksum, and rollback.
- Implement the lowest-risk fixes first: product facts, FAQ/content drafts, JSON-LD or Salla fields only after contract verification.
- Record every change in an activity log and attach a before/after re-test.

**Exit criteria:** a merchant can move from one finding to one approved, traceable change and see the re-test without leaving the workflow.

### Phase 3 — reach GCC sales-agent parity (week 6–12)

**Goal:** convert trustworthy catalog data into a better commercial experience.

- Add PDP-contextual suggested questions in Arabic.
- Add narrow, measurable proactive triggers: time on product, repeated comparison, return visit, and exit intent. Rate-limit and allow opt-out.
- Support product comparison and cart action only through verified Salla primitives.
- Build human handoff with conversation context.
- Make WhatsApp the first external channel; add Instagram or a broader inbox only when merchant evidence supports it.
- Convert repeated unanswered questions and objections into content/catalog opportunities automatically.
- Send a weekly merchant brief: what shoppers asked, what the agent answered, missing information, recommendation/cart behavior, and reconciled revenue evidence.

**Exit criteria:** the agent is correct, observable, and handoff-safe before it becomes more autonomous.

### Phase 4 — production visibility monitoring (week 8–16)

**Goal:** turn observed visibility from a demo into an evidence product.

- Launch a reviewable manual prompt cohort first: fixed questions, language, country, model/provider, date, method, and raw evidence.
- Add provider integrations only where terms and reliability are acceptable.
- Display coverage, failures, and unavailable states explicitly; never score unavailability as a loss.
- Show query-level win/loss and citations only for a stable comparable cohort.
- Keep readiness separate from observed mentions and both separate from revenue causality.
- Attribute AI referral traffic where referrers or tagged links provide evidence; label inference and unknowns.

**Exit criteria:** another reviewer can reproduce what was measured, when, where, and with what coverage.

### Phase 5 — build the proof and distribution moat (ongoing)

**Goal:** turn production evidence into lower-cost acquisition.

- Publish one case study every two weeks during the design-partner phase.
- Create a quarterly “State of Arabic AI Commerce” benchmark using aggregated, permissioned data.
- Publish comparison pages and category guides around Arabic AI visibility, Salla product data, AI shopping questions, and grounded sales agents.
- Maintain the free scan as the acquisition wedge; reveal one meaningful finding before lead capture and reserve the full report for conversion.
- Ask for a Salla marketplace review after a merchant receives a verified result, not immediately after installation.
- Partner with Salla agencies, SEO consultants, CX/WhatsApp providers, and vertical commerce specialists.

## Packaging recommendation

Use outcome-based packaging, not competitor feature copying:

| Offer | Role | Recommendation |
|---|---|---|
| Free scan | Acquisition and education | Instant partial result; no account required before the first useful evidence |
| SAR 399 report | Paid diagnosis | Keep; include evidence, prioritized fixes, owner, and a review call or clear implementation path |
| Implementation sprint | Bridge from report to subscription | Add a 30-day fixed-scope sprint; credit part of the fee toward subscription if the merchant continues |
| SAR 699 growth | Continuous readiness/visibility | Define prompt cohort, rescans, actions, and content limits clearly |
| SAR 1,499 commerce | Managed agent + evidence | Keep only if it includes assisted setup, grounded recommendation controls, weekly intelligence, and verified attribution; otherwise pilot at a lower founding-merchant rate |

Do not use credits unless every action maps transparently to merchant value. Conversation allowances or catalog/store limits are easier to forecast.

## Measurement system

### Activation

- scan completion rate and time to first result;
- preview-to-report conversion;
- report-to-Salla-connect conversion;
- time to catalog ready;
- time to first correct recommendation;
- five launch tests passed;
- widget-live rate.

### Merchant value

- grounded-answer accuracy from an audited sample;
- unanswered/missing-information rate;
- recommendation click-through;
- add-to-cart rate;
- direct and influenced reconciled orders/revenue;
- findings accepted and implemented;
- before/after rescan movement;
- support deflection and successful handoff.

### Trust

- evidence coverage;
- stale fact rate;
- unsupported-claim block rate;
- product/price/stock mismatch rate;
- provider unavailable coverage;
- false observed-mention rate;
- rollback and correction frequency.

### Growth

- free scan to paid report;
- report to subscription;
- 30- and 90-day retention;
- time to first marketplace review;
- referral and partner contribution;
- support time to resolution.

## Priority order

### P0 — do now

1. Verified Salla installation, widget, events, orders, and attribution.
2. Three to five design partners and the first evidence-backed case studies.
3. Unified diagnosis → approved fix → activity log → re-test workflow.
4. Checker-led landing page with a public sample report and vertical demo.

### P1 — after the loop is real

5. Reviewable/compliant observed visibility monitoring.
6. PDP-contextual prompts, narrow proactive selling, and WhatsApp handoff.
7. Safe Salla write-back with diff, versioning, and rollback.

### P2 — expand after evidence

8. Broader integrations and omnichannel inbox.
9. Carefully scoped auto-approval/autopilot.
10. Large benchmark reports, comparison SEO, and partner distribution.
