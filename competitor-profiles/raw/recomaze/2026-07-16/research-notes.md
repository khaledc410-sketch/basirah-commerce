# Recomaze — raw research notes

**Captured:** 2026-07-16  
**Method:** in-app browser review of the Shopify listing, official product site, gallery, pricing, and free-audit entry flow.

## Sources

- Shopify listing: https://apps.shopify.com/recomaze-ai
- Product page: https://recomaze.ai/product
- Free audit entry: https://audit.recomaze.ai/ai-visibility

## Marketplace snapshot

- Shopify rating: 5.0/5 from 6 reviews in the reviewed listing.
- Launched: 5 August 2024.
- Free tier with 300 credits, described as roughly fifty products.
- $39/month for 2,000 credits, described as roughly 100–500 products.
- $109/month for 8,000 credits, described as up to roughly 2,000 products.
- $399/month for 40,000 credits, multilingual support, and customization.
- Seven-day trial on paid tiers.

## Landing page and marketing observations

- Category promise combines two products: an on-site sales agent and AI answer-engine optimization.
- Hero frame: AI is shopping for customers; can it find the merchant?
- The design is black with magenta/purple gradients, dense product imagery, and urgent category-creation copy.
- The journey is compressed into a memorable time sequence: install in a minute, catalog work in the first hour, sales agent live on day one, indexing in a week, and competitive gains after a month. These are marketing claims, not verified service-level guarantees.
- The free audit asks for store URL and email, then promises ten queries, share of voice, competitor comparison, citations, and GEO recommendations.

## Product flow and report observations

1. Install once and ingest the catalog.
2. Run a broad set of catalog and AI-readiness checks.
3. View model- and product-level share of voice.
4. Open a lost query to see the winning competitor and a recommended fix.
5. Apply fixes or bulk rewrite product records.
6. Publish changes.
7. Deploy a catalog-aware sales agent on product pages.
8. Continue weekly diagnostics.

The product gallery showed:

- a product-page sales agent with a product card, add-to-cart action, and remembered size preference;
- model- and product-level share-of-voice views;
- query win/loss against competitors with one-click fixes;
- a bulk rewrite screen with before/after content, scoring, and “publish all” behavior;
- eighty-four weekly diagnostics, a readiness score, and prioritized fixes.

## Strength of the proposition

- It gives the clearest competitive example of “one installation, two engines”: improve AI visibility and convert the resulting on-site demand.
- Every diagnostic is shown near an action, making the product feel operational rather than consultative.
- The time-based setup story is easy for a merchant to remember.

## Evidence cautions

- Marketplace proof is limited to six reviews in the reviewed snapshot.
- Credit pricing obscures the merchant's actual monthly cost until usage is understood.
- The broad commercial and visibility claims are substantially stronger than the public evidence base.
- Bulk edits and wide store permissions create correctness, approval, and rollback risk.
